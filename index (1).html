About

I am currently pursuing a PhD in Computational Mathematics and Statistics at the University of Puerto Rico, Río Piedras. My work lies at the intersection of analysis, geometry, and applied mathematics, with emphasis on partial differential equations over non-smooth and irregular domains.

I am especially interested in fractional Laplacian operators, anomalous diffusion, heat kernels, ramified and fractal structures, and the emerging interface between PDE theory and machine learning methods such as PINNs and operator learning.

  • Partial differential equations and boundary value problems
  • Fractional Laplacian and nonlocal operators
  • Analysis on fractals and ramified domains
  • Diffusion processes and heat flow in complex geometries
  • Functional and classical analysis
  • PDEs and machine learning / operator learning
